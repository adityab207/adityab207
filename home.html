Combating Air Pollution from Crop Residue Burning

ABOUT


Air pollution in India is a significant health and environmental concern, exacerbated by the widespread burning of crop residue in agricultural fields. This practice contributes to the emission of harmful pollutants, leading to poor air quality and adverse health effects. The lack of efficient mechanisms for the disposal of crop residue further compounds the issue.



IMPACT


Health Impact: Crop residue burning releases fine particulate matter (PM2.5) and other pollutants that penetrate deep into the lungs, leading to respiratory and cardiovascular diseases. Vulnerable populations, such as children and the elderly, are especially at risk.


Environmental Impact: The practice contributes to greenhouse gas emissions, affecting climate change, and causes soil degradation, reducing agricultural productivity. Additionally, it results in the loss of valuable nutrients in the soil that are essential for crop growth.
